MATH 2330: Multivariable Calculus
Section 5.6: Applications of Double Integrals

Key Concept:

Given a density function ρ(x,y) that tells how much “stuﬀ” unit area there is at a given point in a region D, then

∬Dρ(x,y)dA

calculates the “total amount of stuﬀ” within region D.
 

Example: If u(x,y) represents the population density in region D, then ∬Du(x,y)dA gives the total population in region D.
 

Center of Mass of a Lamina:

mass density function: ρ(x,y) has units mass area
 
total mass of the lamina: m = ∬Dρ(x,y)dA
 
Center of mass: (x̄,ȳ)
 
x̄ = 1 m∬Dρ(x,y)xdA
 
ȳ = 1 m∬Dρ(x,y)ydA

Example:

Find the center of mass of the lamina D that has density function ρ(x,y) = x2, where D is the triangular region bounded by x = 0,y = x,2x + y = 6.
