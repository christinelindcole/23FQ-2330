Section 5.1: Double Integrals over Rectangular Regions

Double Integral Deﬁnition:

     

Figure 1: Double Integral Deﬁnition Diagrams

A double integral over a rectangle R : [a,b] × [c,d] is deﬁned as:

∬Rf(x,y)dA = limmaxΔxi,Δyj→0 ∑ i=0m ∑ j=0nf x ij∗,y ij∗ΔA ij

 
Key Idea:

  • Split the rectangle into mn sub-rectangles of area
    ΔAij = ΔxiΔyj
  • Pick a sample point from each sub-rectangle: xij∗,yij∗
  • Evaluate the function at each sample point: f xij∗,yij∗
  • Calculate the volume of a column: V ij = f xij∗,yij∗ΔAij
  • Add up the volumes of the columns to estimate the total volume: V ≈ V 11 + V 12 + … + V mn

Fubini’s Theorem:

If f(x,y) is continuous on R = {(x,y)|a ≤ x ≤ b,c ≤ y ≤ d} then:

∬Rf(x,y)dA = ∫ cd ∫ abf(x,y)dxdy = ∫ ab ∫ cdf(x,y)dydx

Key Ideas:

  • Order of Operations: work from the inside out (like parentheses!)
  • We can swap the order of integration in the iterated integrals
  • The ﬁnal result should be a number, no x’s or y’s should remain
  • Partial Integration:
    • ∫ abf(x,y)dx: hold y ﬁxed and integrate wrt x.
    • ∫ cdf(x,y)dy: hold x ﬁxed and integrate wrt y.
