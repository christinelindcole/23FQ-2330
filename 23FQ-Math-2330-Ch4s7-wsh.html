Section 4.7 - Maximum & Minimum Values

Types of Critical Points:

∙ Local Minimum ∙ Local Maximum ∙ Saddle Point

First Derivative Test:

A point (a,b) is a critical point of f if:

fx(a,b) = 0andfy(a,b) = 0

or if one of the partial derivatives does not exist at (a,b).

Geometric Interpretation: If there is a local max or min where the tangent plane is deﬁned, the tangent plane will be horizontal.

Second Derivative Test:

In order to classify critical points, we calculate the quantity D:

D = fxxfxy fyx fyy = fxxfyy−fxy 2

There are four possible outcomes from the 2nd derivative test, when D has been evaluated at the critical point (a,b):

Case 1:
D(a,b) > 0, and fxx(a,b) > 0: Local Minimum at (a,b)
Case 2:
D(a,b) > 0, and fxx(a,b) < 0: Local Maximum at (a,b)
Case 3:
D(a,b) < 0: Saddle Point at (a,b)
Case 4:
D(a,b) = 0: Inconclusive, Test Fails ⇒ try something else!

Global (Absolute) Maximum & Minimum Values:

To ﬁnd the absolute max/min values on a closed region D, assemble the candidates, then choose the largest/smallest:

1.
Find the local maxima & minima inside the region D.
2.
Find extreme values on the boundary of D.
3.
The largest value is the global maximum and the smallest value is the global minimum.

Example Problems:

Example 1:
Find and classify all of the critical points of f(x,y) = 2x3 + y3 − 3x2 − 12x − 3y
Example 2:
Find and classify all of the critical points of f(x,y) = x2y4
Example 3:
Find and classify all of the critical points of f(x,y) = x2 + 4xy + 3y2 − 6x − 12y
Example 4:
Find the global maximum and minimum values of f(x,y) = x2y4 on the domain D = x2 + y2 ≤ 1.
